Tech For Good Ideas

1. BenefitBridge
Concept
A simple assistant that helps users understand which public benefits or aid programs they may qualify for based on a short questionnaire.

Why judges may love it
Clear social impact, easy story, and useful for real communities.

Why it is easy enough
The MVP can be a guided form and recommendation engine without trying to automate applications.

MVP
- short eligibility questionnaire
- likely matching programs
- checklist of needed documents
- save/share results

Wow factor
Confusing support systems become understandable in a few steps.

2. ShelterSync
Concept
A lightweight dashboard showing nearby shelters, food pantries, and support locations with real-time notes or capacity updates.

Why judges may love it
Immediate community impact and easy to visualize.

Why it is easy enough
Map plus resource cards plus simple admin update mode.

MVP
- searchable map
- resource list
- open/closed or full/available status
- mobile-friendly layout

Wow factor
Critical resources become visible and usable in one place.

3. CoverageLift
Concept
An app that helps small healthcare teams organize denied claims and prepare better appeal workflows.

Why judges may love it
Strong mission, clear real-world value, and healthcare equity angle.

Why it is easy enough
The MVP can focus only on summary, checklist, and workflow support.

MVP
- upload denial letter
- extract key info
- show missing docs
- generate appeal checklist

Wow factor
Administrative complexity gets turned into clear action for understaffed teams.
