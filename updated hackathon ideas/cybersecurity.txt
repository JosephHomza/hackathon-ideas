Cybersecurity Ideas

1. PhishProof QR
Concept
A web app that scans a QR code before the user opens it, expands redirects, checks the destination for phishing or malware risk, and explains why the code looks safe or suspicious.

Why judges may love it
It is easy to understand, timely, security-focused, and highly demo-friendly. Judges can scan or upload a QR code and instantly see a visible risk analysis rather than a vague security score.

Why it is easy enough
The MVP can stay narrow:
- upload or camera scan a QR code
- decode the URL
- expand shortened links
- check risk signals
- show a safety verdict with explanation

MVP
- upload QR image or use webcam
- decode QR destination
- show final URL and redirect path
- check for phishing or malware indicators
- show verdict: safe, caution, suspicious, or dangerous
- display reasons such as lookalike domain, suspicious TLD, or redirect chain

Wow factor
The user scans a normal-looking QR code and the app reveals the hidden destination and risk story behind it.

Best hackathon framing
This is stronger if pitched as more than a scanner. Position it as a trust layer for QR codes that makes invisible threats visible before the user clicks.

Best website pitch
PhishProof QR helps people inspect QR codes before they trust them. It shows where the code really leads, whether it contains phishing or malware risk, and why.

Stretch features
- branded safe QR generator
- domain reputation summary
- anti-tamper or trusted-business mode
- educational attack walkthrough

2. LinkLens
Concept
A site where users paste suspicious links and get a visual breakdown of redirects, risky patterns, and why a domain may be unsafe.

Why judges may love it
The analysis is visual and educational, which makes the demo clear.

Why it is easy enough
No QR support needed for the first version, so the scope stays simpler.

MVP
- paste link
- expand redirects
- show domain summary
- flag suspicious signals

Wow factor
An innocent-looking link turns into a visible chain of risk.

3. InboxGuard Demo
Concept
A website that takes a suspicious email screenshot or pasted content and highlights signs of phishing for the user.

Why judges may love it
Very relatable and easy to test live.

Why it is easy enough
The MVP can focus on rule-based and AI-assisted analysis without trying to become a full email security product.

MVP
- upload screenshot or paste email text
- highlight urgency language, spoofed sender clues, suspicious links, and brand mimicry
- show a simple safety verdict

Wow factor
The app visually teaches users how scams work in seconds.
